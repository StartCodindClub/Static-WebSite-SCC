Our Certificate & Online Programs For 2021

Students can earn certificates through qualifying, non-degree courses of study at colleges, universities, and technical schools. Postsecondary certificates are a popular option, likely because they take significantly less time to complete than degrees.This guide explores the best online certificate programs and the reasons that certificates are so popular. The following sections review how to transfer a certificate into a degree program and provide information about accreditation for students earning certificates online.


However, several factors, such as rapid technological advancement and increasing globalization, have impacted the current educational landscape in the United States. An increasing number of students opt for alternative academic options, including pursuing an online certificate program. Enrolling in a certificate program offers several advantages in terms of time, affordabilit, and professional options. You can read about these advantages in greater detail below. This guide provides information that can help you decide whether enrolling in an online certificate program is right for you. Learn more about some of the most popular certificate programs available to students today. Before making a final decision, explore key aspects of certificate programs, including accreditation, cost, and school ranking. Continue reading below to learn how you can benefit from the academic and career advantages of online certificate programs.


There are currently three types of higher education accreditation in the United States. Regional accreditation focuses on degree-oriented nonprofit institutions, which may be public or private schools. For-profit trade or sectarian schools often seek national accreditation. The third type, programmatic accreditation, focuses on specific programs offered by schools that hold national or regional accreditation. The U.S. Department of Education (ED) and the Council for Higher Education Accreditation (CHEA) oversee the accrediting process and make sure accrediting agencies apply the same rigorous standards to all the schools and programs that fall under their rightful jurisdiction. Accreditation signifies more than quality education. Students can only receive federal financial assista if they are enrolled in an accredited institution. Accreditation also matters when transferring credits. Schools with regional accreditation only accept credits earned at another institution that holds the same type of accreditation. They seldom accept credits earned at a school with national accreditation.


In evaluating online programs, consider those offered by an accredited school. If you are looking for an online school (and not just a brick-and-mortar campus offering online programs), look for accreditation by the Distance Education Accrediting Commission (DEAC). ED and CHEA recognize DEAC as the main accreditor for online schools.
